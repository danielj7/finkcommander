Updating Table Data

On startup and sometimes after a command is processed, a progress indicator will appear in the lower right hand corner of the FinkCommander window, and the message "Updating table data . . ." will appear on the left. This means that FinkCommander is running apt-get and fink commands to obtain the information about available packages displayed in the table. The process is similar to the one Fink itself engages in when you see the message "Reading package info . . ." in the terminal. Completing this process necessarily takes some time, although Fink 0.4.0 users may notice a substantial speed increase compared to earlier versions.
To reduce the time taken to update the table after a command, turn off "Always update with fink" in Preferences:Commander. FinkCommander will attempt to update the table data itself after a command (other than selfupdate or selfupdate-cvs) is performed. The selfupdate commands always result in a complete update of the table.
FinkCommander's update method is faster (pretty much instantaneous) but less accurate than Fink's. For example, if installing a package results in the installation of dependencies, FinkCommander will only update the status of the selected package.
You can force FinkCommander to do a complete update of the table at any time by selecting File:Update Table Data.
